Mission Beach is one of San Diego's most distinct neighborhoods — a narrow strip of land between the Pacific Ocean and Mission Bay, packed with boardwalk culture, surf shops, beach rentals, and some of the city's best casual dining and drinking.

## The Beach and the Boardwalk

The Mission Beach boardwalk runs the length of the neighborhood along the Pacific side. Bike rentals, rollerblades, and morning runs. Belmont Park's vintage Giant Dipper roller coaster is the neighborhood's most recognizable landmark and worth a lap.

## Eat and Drink on Mission Blvd

Mission Blvd is the neighborhood's main commercial street with a dense concentration of restaurants, bars, and coffee shops. Miss B's Coconut Club at 3704 Mission Blvd is the standout — open daily from 11am, brunch on weekends from 10am.

## Miss B's Coconut Club

No Mission Beach itinerary is complete without a stop at Miss B's. The open-air bar, the tropical dining room, the flamingo taps — it's the neighborhood's most photogenic venue and consistently ranks among the best bars in San Diego.
